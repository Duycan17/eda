# Project Motivation

## Motivations

- Railway operators receive large volumes of passenger reviews, but most of this feedback is unstructured and difficult to use directly for business decisions.
- Passenger dissatisfaction is usually caused by multiple service aspects, not a single issue.
- Common pain points such as `delays`, `crowding`, `ticketing friction`, `cleanliness`, `staff support`, and `station accessibility` need to be identified more clearly.
- A simple `positive` or `negative` label is not enough for management.
- Operators need to know which service aspects create the most complaints, where those problems happen, and what should be fixed first.
- The project follows the course guideline of turning raw text data into a strategic business narrative and actionable consulting recommendations.
- The project plan also positions the work as `Aspect-Based Sentiment Analysis (ABSA)` so that insights can be tied to specific service touchpoints and management actions.

## Convert Raw Feedback into Consulting Insights

- The project turns unstructured passenger reviews into findings that railway operators can use for service improvement decisions.

#### Example

- Instead of only saying that sentiment is negative, the project can show that most complaints come from `delays` and `crowding` at major interchange stations.

## Identify the Main Customer Pain Points

- The project helps reveal which service aspects create the strongest dissatisfaction among passengers.

#### Example

- Reviews may show that passengers complain more about `waiting time` and `platform congestion` than about ticket price or station cleanliness.

## Show Where Problems Are Concentrated

- The project can compare different stations or touchpoints to locate where service quality issues are most severe.

#### Example

- The analysis can compare stations such as Siam, Asok, or Mo Chit and reveal which location receives the highest share of negative reviews.

## Track Dissatisfaction Over Time

- The project can monitor sentiment trends and detect periods when customer dissatisfaction becomes more serious.

#### Example

- Sentiment trends may show that negative reviews rise during peak hours, holiday periods, or disruption events.

## Support Better Management Prioritization

- The project gives management measurable evidence for deciding which issues should be addressed first.

#### Example

- If `accessibility` complaints are fewer in number but strongly negative, management may still prioritize them because they carry higher business and service risk.

## Provide Executive-Friendly Metrics and Visuals

- The project supports decision-making through business-oriented outputs rather than raw model scores alone.

#### Example

- `Net Sentiment Score (NSS)`, trend charts, word clouds, and aspect-level summaries can be used in a dashboard for reporting.

## Strengthen Strategic Recommendations

- The project creates a stronger basis for recommendations by linking actions directly to passenger feedback patterns.

#### Example

- The project can recommend increasing crowd management at busy stations, improving ticketing flow, or focusing service recovery on the aspects with the highest complaint volume.

## Deliver a Practical Decision-Support Tool

- The final value of the project is to support executive action, not only to compare model performance.

#### Example

- The final outcome is not just which model performs better, but what railway operators should improve first and why.
